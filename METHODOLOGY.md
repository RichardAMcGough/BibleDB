# Allusion Analysis Methodology

**Foundational instructions for evaluating intertextual allusions in the Isaiah–Bible Correlation (IBC) study.**
Grounded in Swale (*Three Instincts*), Hays (*seven criteria*), Leonard (*inner-biblical allusion*), and Sandmel (*parallelomania*).

---

## Prime Directive

**Truth alone.** We test whether a *real* pattern exists — one that would survive scrutiny by a billion minds over unlimited time. We are fearlessly honest about every fact, whether it supports or weakens the IBC. We do not confirm a thesis; we find what is there. A negative result is as valuable as a positive one and must be reported with equal force.

---

## Two Questions, Kept Strictly Separate

- **Q1 — Allusion:** Is there a genuine intertextual connection between two passages?
- **Q2 — The IBC:** Does the chapter ↔ book numerical correspondence predict where allusions occur, beyond chance?

**Q1 evidence never counts as Q2 evidence without the statistical control (below).** A brilliant allusion proves an allusion; it does not, by itself, prove the numbering.

---

## Core Stance: Pure Pattern Detection, No Bias About the Agent

We make **no presupposition about who or what produced any pattern** — human author, scribe, canonical arranger, or transcendent mind. The agent is an open variable.

Therefore we **remove two of Hays's criteria**, because they presuppose the agent's identity and temporal/epistemic limits and so inject extreme bias about the ultimate cause:

- ✗ **Availability (Hays #1)** — "could *this human, at this date,* access the source?"
- ✗ **Historical plausibility (Hays #5)** — "could *this human* have intended it?"

Dating and direction of dependence remain *facts about the texts*. They are **data held for Stage B**, never filters that discount a detected pattern.

---

## Stage A — Detection & Scoring (agency-neutral)

Adjudicate each proposed link by the following, **in priority order**:

1. **Shared language is the gate** *(Leonard 1, 3, 4, 5; Hays: Volume).*
   Distinctive/rare language ≫ common; shared **phrases** ≫ single words; **density/accumulation** strengthens. Rarity = frequency across the **whole canon**, not within one query.
   → **No distinctive shared language = "echo" at most. Cannot be graded strong, however rich the theme.** This rule is the primary guard against cherry-picking.

2. **Context & coherence** *(Leonard 6; Hays: Thematic coherence; Sandmel).*
   Read full pericopes, never excerpted snippets. Does the shared language sit in a comparable argument?

3. **Thesis / purpose** *(Swale's third instinct).*
   Does the connection do **specifiable work the text would lose without it?** Inert co-occurrence is not allusion. *Subordinate to the language gate — purpose strengthens a verbal link; it never rescues a theme-only parallel.*

4. **Recurrence / accumulation** *(Hays #3, reframed neutrally).*
   Multiple independent links between the same pair strengthen the case.

5. **Independent attestation** *(Hays #6).*
   Is the link recognized in commentaries/scholarship? **Novel-only sightings carry a skepticism discount** — "only I can see it" is the signature of selection bias.

6. **Satisfaction** *(Hays #7).*
   Does reading the two together genuinely illuminate both?

**Typology** (descending evidential weight): **Quotation > Allusion > Echo.**
**Direction of dependence:** state it where relevant; it is data, not a verdict.
**The verdict may be NEGATIVE.** Maintain a running tally of **hits and misses** — without the denominator, nothing is proven.

---

## Stage B — Explanation (only after a pattern is detected; still no default)

Weigh **all** candidate causes neutrally — chance, scribal/canonical arrangement, human allusion (either direction), transcendent design. Adjudicate by improbability and the textual facts. **No candidate gets home-field advantage — neither naturalistic nor theistic.**

---

## The Decisive Q2 Test: Statistical Control

Removing the agent-bias criteria shifts the entire burden onto improbability. A chapter ↔ book result is evidence for the IBC **only if the true pairing's strength exceeds matched RANDOM control pairings.** Report the **margin over control**, not the raw score. This is the test that converts allusion-hunting into a real experiment.

---

## Standing Anti-Bias Safeguards

- **Blind first pass:** detect connections *without* priming the expected verses or parallel.
- **No hand-picking:** do not select target verses to fit a hoped-for result; search neutrally.
- **Same-author discount:** intra-book / same-author comparisons share vocabulary by default and are weak tests. The **self-cell** (a chapter mapping to its own book) is degenerate.
- **Clean lexical data:** filter morphological markers (e.g., OSHB H9xxx); never treat grammatical particles as shared "terms."
- **Lead with disconfirmation:** report the facts that weaken a connection first.
