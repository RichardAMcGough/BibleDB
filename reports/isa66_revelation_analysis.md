## Connection 1 — Isa 66:22-23 // Rev 21:1-5 | Grade AAA+ | Score 26

**"New heavens and new earth" — the only verbatim formula in Scripture**

The new-creation formula is a near-exact LXX quotation found in **only these two places** in all of Scripture:

| LXX Isa 66:22 | Rev 21:1 |
|---|---|
| ὁ **οὐρανὸς καινὸς καὶ ἡ γῆ καινή** | **οὐρανὸν καινὸν καὶ γῆν καινήν** |
| *the new heaven and the new earth* | *a new heaven and a new earth* |

The climactic verbal anchor of the whole canon. Isaiah 66:22's "remain/stand before me" (permanence of the new creation and of Israel's offspring/name) is enacted in Rev 21:1-5: the first heaven and earth pass away, and the One on the throne declares "Behold, I make all things new (καινὰ ποιῶ πάντα)." Isaiah 66:23's "all flesh shall come to worship" finds its dwelling-place fulfillment in Rev 21:3: "the dwelling of God is with man... they shall be his peoples."

## Connection 2 — Isa 66:7-9 // Rev 12:1-5 | Grade AA+ | Score 22

**Zion gives birth to a male child before labor**

The shared LXX anchor is **ἄρσεν (arsen, "male")**. Isaiah 66:7 LXX: "before she labored she gave birth... she delivered a **male** (ἄρσεν)." Revelation 12:5: "she bore a **son, a male** (υἱὸν ἄρσεν)." The phrase *huion arsen* in Rev 12:5 is grammatically redundant in Greek ("a son, a male") — awkward precisely because John fuses his "son" language with Isaiah 66:7's distinctive **ἄρσεν**.

| Isa 66:7-8 | Rev 12:2,5 |
|---|---|
| "before her pain came, she delivered a **male**" | "crying out in **birth pains** (ὠδίνουσα)... she bore a son, a **male**" |
| "shall a **nation** be born in one moment?" | "to rule all the **nations** (ἔθνη) with an iron rod" |
| Zion gives sudden miraculous birth | the cosmic woman gives birth; child caught up to God's throne |

The sudden-miraculous-birth motif (a nation born "in one day") is uniquely Isaiah 66; the iron-rod rule is Psalm 2. John composites them. The child "caught up to God and to his throne" corresponds to Isaiah's miraculous deliverance.

**Scores:** Terms 5 | Themes 9 | Thesis 8 | Total 22  
**LXX anchor:** ἄρσεν (male) shared verbatim; ὠδίν/birth-pain vocabulary; ἔθνη  
**Themes:** Sudden miraculous birth; Zion/the woman as mother of the messianic son and the nation; birth before/without labor; cosmic significance of the child  
**Thesis:** Revelation 12's pivotal birth-vision draws its "male child" language and sudden-birth motif from Isaiah 66:7. Without it, the redundant "son, a male" is inexplicable.

## The Structural Capstone

Isaiah 66 functions as a source-text quarry for Revelation's entire consummation:

- The **new heavens and new earth** (66:22 → Rev 21:1) — verbatim LXX
- The **birth of the male child / nation** (66:7 → Rev 12:5) — distinctive *arsen*
- The **throne in heaven / no temple** (66:1 → Rev 4; 21:22)
- The **fiery sword-judgment on all flesh** (66:15-16 → Rev 19)
- The **gathering of the nations bringing glory** (66:18-21 → Rev 21:24-26)
- **Jerusalem as comforting mother, end of sorrow** (66:10-14 → Rev 21:2-4)
- The **undying worm and unquenched fire** (66:24 → Rev 14; 20)

On the Bible Wheel, Isaiah chapter 66 corresponds to Revelation, book #66 — the final chapter of the prophet to the final book of the canon.
